Where LTV Gets Built and Lost at Helfy

An outside-in view on turning episodic care into compounding revenue.

Helfy has already solved acquisition at scale: 1M+ users driven through high-performance, condition-based funnels.

The constraint is not growth but continuity. Converting episodic, condition-driven usage into repeat behavior and structured lifetime value.

The opportunity is to build lifecycle infrastructure that captures the inherent recurrence already present in these conditions.

Helfy behaves like a transaction platform in a category where value is created through continuity.

Where retention breaks
  • 1Telehealth is episodic: industry retention averages ~30% annually
  • 2Condition resolution drives natural churn: patient feels better, disappears
  • 3No lifecycle converting inherent recurrence into repeat habit
  • 4Loyalty and subscription nascent: flagged in VP JD as being built now
  • 5Multi-market complexity fragments execution
What is already working
  • 1Acquisition solved: 1M+ users, strong paid engine, 128% traffic growth YoY
  • 2Chronic-condition verticals (ED, hair loss, weight, acne) create inherent recurrence
  • 3UK, France, Switzerland are OTC-only: frictionless repeat purchase, no consultation needed
5 levers to unlock LTV

All five focus on one shift: from transaction triggers to continuity systems.

01
OTC markets are a retention arbitrage hiding in plain sight
Zero-friction reorder already exists in UK, France, and Switzerland. The gap is converting that into habit. The constraint is not mechanics, but trust. Retention in health must be built through clinical relevance, not behavioral pressure.
02
LTV is predictable at day one: segment early by condition type
A chronic-condition buyer is structurally different from a one-time acute buyer. Segment at first purchase. The high-LTV cohort needs a different engagement motion from the start, not a recovery motion after churn.
03
Loyalty should track health progress, not spend
In health, retention follows perceived progress, not discounts. Adherence milestones and health goals create stickiness a discount scheme cannot. It also positions DoktorABC as a long-term health partner, not a one-time shortcut.
04
Cross-condition expansion is the revenue engine
A patient treating ED is a strong candidate for weight, cardiovascular health, and sleep. Helfy's CEO framed it: "No infection comes alone." Adjacent conditions surfaced as proactive health suggestions, not ads, are where LTV compounds.
05
One strategy, four market-specific execution playbooks
OTC markets support direct re-engagement. Germany's prescription track requires framing the consultation as care continuity, not a purchase barrier. One strategic framework, locally adapted across UK, France, Switzerland, and Germany.

Contrarian insight

Patients do not return because of reminders or discounts.

They return when the platform becomes part of their ongoing health narrative. The retention infrastructure Helfy needs is not a CRM campaign layer. It is a value perception layer that makes every interaction feel like care, not commerce.

With acquisition already working at scale, Helfy does not need more customers.

It needs to extract more value from the ones it already has.

That shift, from transactions to continuity, is where LTV compounds.
